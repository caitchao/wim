Body Image

Do your hair differently.
Your makeup looks weird.
Lose some weight.
Wear this dress, it will bring out your curves.
You don't want him to see you like that, do you?
❮ ❯

In our society, people tend to tell you what you should or should not be- how you should look like, how you should act properly, etc. Because of the social standards that are enforced upon the world, people think that this is actually normal and this is what they are supposed to do. Any mere difference or deviance from this set standard is considered as abnormal, and conseqeuntly leads to people's insecurities about themselves. These standards are seen a lot in the media, which idealizes the "skinny supermodel body". People these days idealize the large breasts and butts, tiny waists, and the appearance of curves. People will praise you for having thick curves or a skinny body but shame you for not.

Because most movies and commercials in the media these days convey images of females with the "perfect body type", the general population regards this as the norm. But in reality, the images displayed by the media are not representative of the general population. People trust the media thinking that it is the reality and so, any slight difference takes a large toll on them. Far-reaching effects may occur because of this, such as life-threatening eating disorders, like Anorexia or Bulimia. The consequences that result from this false portrayal from the media is usually disregarded, as more and more people are following these idealized ways.

Have you ever used Facetune or any other beauty editing apps? Why is it that these apps make people edit their body shapes differently, make their eyes appear larger, or make their skin appear smoother? Two different images of two completely different people may end up looking like the same person with the use of these applications. And what does that person look like? The same image that is praised by the media and society and is falsely displayed across the world. People want to look like everyone else they see on shows or advertisements. Moreover, the media tends to promote these apps, and from seeing everyone, including social influencers, use these apps, people want to join in the crowd. But what is the point of showing the world who you are not, and hiding who you actually are? Nobody will distinguish your unique attributes and personalities from seeing a false image of you. Furthermore, if everyone looked the same way, like the media tends to portray, the world would be a very boring place.

Another type of media portrayal of false body imaging is through the stark comparison of different characters. You will see that there is one character, who is the popular chic and the one everyone wants to be friends with at school. Her apparel is either boujee or one of the latest trends on the market. She (unrealistically) works out all the time, which attributes her perfect body type, where she is able to fit into the smallest sizes of clothes. Her skin is naturally perfect without any makeup, and she never breaks out. Then there is the complete foil of her- the girl who seems to disappear among the social crowds, and the one who is looked down upon by the popular groups. She dresses somewhat ordinarily and doesn't put on any extravagant image of herself. But she is supposed to be the "real" portrayal of ordinary people. This clear comparison is used by the media to categorize people into what they want to be like versus what they seem like ordinarily. This is very destructive on a person's self esteem because they are seeing society tell them that they are not worthy, and that they are supposed to be a certain way.

Have you ever seen a movie or show where the female gets a makeover and suddenly she is the most beautiful girl to grace the earth? Where was this attention to the "before" image? Are appearances all that matter to society, that people ignore their true personalities? Essentially, she is the same girl, because her personality and values remain the same. All that it takes is a change in appearance for the world to view her in a completely different way. One example is the drama called "Diamond Lover," where the female lead was forced to undergo facial reconstruction due to a car accident. The male lead falls in love with her new image and doesn't know her past. When he finally finds out about her past- she was an obese girl before, he completely leaves her and shames her for ever undergoing plastic surgery. But she had no choice but to do it. This truly illustrates the extent to which people only care about appearances, and reject a person's inner qualities.
