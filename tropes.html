Female Tropes

Do you oftentimes watch movies or read books that have "typical" female characters with "typical" qualities? Like that girl who is crazy for crushing on a guy, or that lady who only cares about her romantic endeavors? Some of these stereotypes are deemed normal in today's society, and are even used as sources of satire and humor. People grow up watching these outlets of media, and consequently, they grow up thinking that these tropes are real and just. But these ways of thinking are merely enforced upon our society, and we have the power to change the way we perceive females in our world.

When a woman shows her strength and prowess, she is immediately thought of as "badass". Others do not consider that women do possess skills and that they do not sit still and look pretty as some people perceive they do. People do not realize the adeptness of females because they are so adament about their preconceptions. They put into their minds that women should not work, they should not show how strong they are, and that they should just be elegant and graceful. When females rise to the top positions of their careers, they are also thought of as heartless or cunning, and they are misjudged for using deceptive or romantic ways to reach their points.

The Damsel in Distress

Frequently, the media depict women as people who wait for the man to come to the rescue and save them. They are portrayed as weak, frail, and helpless. They cannot prevail alone without the assistance of a man. In movies, they are sometimes abducted or abused, and they wait for the man to rescue them, without doing anything themselves. Other times, the media may even portray the woman falling in love with the man who abducted her.

The "Boy Crazy"

Many people oftentimes think that women cannot have a simple conversation without talking about men. There is a presumption that women would go out of their way to impress a male figure, whether it is through changing their appearance, seduction, or other means. People are also wary of women- they classify some women as gold-diggers who want men for their money and have greedy intentions. Even a mere crush becomes a means of "stalking." The double standards are evident as men are rewarded for their intentions of protecting women when they are after a girl.
